# <h2 style="color:darkblue; font:Times New Roman;">Phase 0: Data Collection — Step 01</h2> 
<br>

## **Aim**  
To systematically obtain high-quality genomes, proteomes, CDS, and specific gene annotations for both our model species and selected angiosperms. Having robust and verified sequence databases is critical before launching our evolutionary search.
<br>

## Steps:-

> ### Step 1: Identify the Required Structural Files
Before performing any scripting, we must download the complete sequence profiles for each species to ensure our domain matching is accurate.

| Data Type | Purpose | Format Focus |
|-----------|------------------------------|----------------|
| **Whole Genome** | Genomic context & synteny check | `.fa / .fna` |
| **Proteome** | Domain search, BLAST mapping, & MSA | `.fa / .faa` |
| **CDS** | Coding sequence validation | `.cds.fa` |
| **Transcripts** | Optional RNA-seq mapping | `.cdna.fa` |
| **Annotation** | Gene coordinates & models | `.gff3` |

<br>

> ### Step 2: Establish the Anchoring System
**Arabidopsis thaliana** serves as our primary model reference species in this workflow. Because its ABCDE floral genes are experimentally validated and well-characterized, we use its curated TAIR dataset as the master evolutionary coordinate system.

<br>

> ### Step 3: Download the Datasets
Retrieve the public datasets explicitly from Phytozome, TAIR, or NCBI. Always prefer the **primary transcript/protein** file sets to avoid isoform redundancy right out of the gate!

**Included Species Overview:**
* **Basal Angiosperms:** *Amborella trichopoda* (v1.0), *Nymphaea colorata*
* **Magnoliids:** *Cinnamomum kanehirae* (v3)
* **Monocots:** *Oryza sativa* (IRGSP-1.0)
* **Rosids:** *Glycine max* (Wm82.a4.v1), *Medicago truncatula* (Mt4.0), *Prunus persica* (v2.0+)
* **Asterids / Eudicots:** *Helianthus annuus* (Ha412HOv2), *Nelumbo nucifera*

*(Note: Species like Magnolia grandiflora and Piper auritum were systematically excluded due to missing nuclear genomes or incomplete GFF3 annotations).*

<br>

> ### Step 4: Retrieve Pfam Signature Profiles
Finally, download the exact mathematically validated Hidden Markov Models (HMMs) that define our key interaction domains directly from InterPro/Pfam:

| Target Domain | Pfam ID | Required File |
|------------|-----------|-------------|
| **MADS** | PF00319 | `PF00319.hmm` |
| **K-box** | PF01486 | `PF01486.hmm` |
| **AP2** | PF00847 | `PF00847.hmm` |
