# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Cinnamomum kanehirae (Magnoliid)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Cinnamomum kanehirae* shows an intermediate level of MADS-box diversification, reflecting its evolutionary position as a Magnoliid.

### **AP1/FUL Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `CKA_01235_MADS`, `CKA_02241_MADS`, `CKA_00982_MADS`
*   **Observations**: This clade shows a distinct bipartite split.
*   **Pattern**: **Basal Divergence** identified. `CKA_01235_MADS` branches basally, while `CKA_02241_MADS` and `CKA_00982_MADS` form a tight Cluster. 

### **SEP3 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `CKA_01567_MADS`, `CKA_02110_MADS`
*   **Observations**: These are highly conserved SEP-like orthologs, displaying a simple 1:2 duplication pattern relative to Arabidopsis.

### **SOC1 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `CKA_00456_MADS`
*   **Observations**: A single ortholog maintained from ancestral angiosperms.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **Magnoliid Evolution and Polyploidy**
While *Cinnamomum* lacks the core eudicot $\gamma$ event, it has undergone its own lineage-specific whole-genome duplications (WGDs). These events shaped the diversification of MIKCc-type genes, which play key roles in the development of its aromatic flowers and complex perianth.

### **Why this happens**:
The "Basal Divergence" pattern in the AP1/FUL clade (e.g., `CKA_01235_MADS` Separate vs the Cluster) is a classic signature of post-duplication sub-functionalization. One copy likely diverged to regulate vegetative growth/leaf development ( ancestral FUL function), while the derived "Together" cluster specialized in floral organ identity (derived AP1 function).

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The Magnoliid MADS-box profile occupies a critical evolutionary "bridge." Like *Amborella*, it maintains ancestral singleton copies (e.g., SOC1), but like *Rice* and *Nymphaea*, it shows the beginnings of lineage-specific clade expansions through WGD, demonstrating the stepwise progression toward floral complexity.
