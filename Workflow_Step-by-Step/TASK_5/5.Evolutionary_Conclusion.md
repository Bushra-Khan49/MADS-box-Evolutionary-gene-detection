# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Glycine max (Soybean)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Glycine max* is characterized by massive gene family expansions, the result of a highly polyploid history.

### **SOC1 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Glyma_15G106600.1_MADS`, `Glyma_05G242400.1_MADS`, `Glyma_03G120100.1_MADS`, `Glyma_01G090100.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: Quadruplicate expansion relative to Arabidopsis.
*   **Pattern**: **Basal Divergence** identified. `Glyma_15G106600.1_MADS` branches basally (Separate), while the other three form a derived group (Together).

### **AP1/FUL Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Glyma_08G012300.1_MADS`, `Glyma_02G098200.1_MADS`, `Glyma_04G022400.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: High levels of redundancy consistently observed across floral organ identity clades.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **Multiple WGD Events ($\gamma, \omega$, and Soybean-Specific)**
The MADS-box diversification in Soybean is the product of three distinct layers of polyploidy:
1.  **$\gamma$ (gamma) Event**: The ancestral core eudicot triplication (~120 Mya).
2.  **$\omega$ (omega) Event**: The legume-specific WGD (~58 Mya).
3.  **Glycine-specific WGD**: A recent duplication (~13 Mya).

### **Why this happens**:
The "Separate vs Together" pattern in the SOC1 clade (e.g., `Glyma_15G106600.1` vs the cluster) represents the genomic legacy of these events. The basal "Separate" gene likely traces back to the older $\omega$ or $\gamma$ events, having already diverged functionally, while the "Together" cluster represents the more recent, near-identical paralogs resulting from the 13 Mya Glycine-specific WGD.

---

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The extreme redundancy in *Glycine* is mirrored in *Medicago* (another legume), where the $\omega$ event similarly expanded the MADS-box toolbox. However, *Glycine* shows even higher complexity due to its additional recent WGD, making it one of the most redundant MADS-box systems in our study.
