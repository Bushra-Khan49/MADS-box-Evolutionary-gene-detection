# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Medicago truncatula (Barrel Medic)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Medicago truncatula* displays a complex MADS-box family structure, largely influenced by ancestral legume-specific polyploidy.

### **SOC1 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `MtrunA17Chr1g01235_MADS`, `MtrunA17Chr5g09820_MADS`, `MtrunA17Chr7g02241_MADS`
*   **Observations**: This clade shows significant expansion relative to Arabidopsis.
*   **Pattern**: **Basal Divergence** identified. `MtrunA17Chr1g01235_MADS` branches basally (Separate), while the other IDs form a derived group (Together).

### **AP1/FUL Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `MtrunA17Chr3g00456_MADS`, `MtrunA17Chr2g01567_MADS`
*   **Observations**: Orthologous mapping shows 1:2 expansion in the FUL-like lineage.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **The $\omega$ (omega) WGD**
MADS-box genes in *Medicago* were primarily expanded by the **Legume-specific whole-genome duplication ($\omega$ event)**, which occurred approximately 58 million years ago. Unlike *Glycine max*, *Medicago* did not undergo the more recent soybean-specific WGD, making its gene families slightly less redundant but more specialized.

### **Why this happens**:
The "Separate vs Together" pattern in the SOC1 clade (e.g., `MtrunA17Chr1g01235_MADS` vs the cluster) marks the genomic divergence that followed the $\omega$ event. One copy likely diverged to manage specialized legume reproductive traits, such as nodule-linked regulation or specific pod development, while others retained conservative floral transition roles.

---

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The $\omega$-driven expansion in *Medicago* is a direct mirror of the earlier diversification rounds seen in *Glycine*. Across both legume species, the SOC1 and AP1/FUL clades show consistent "Basal Divergence" patterns, suggesting that these lineages are key "evolutionary hubs" for diversification in the Fabaceae.
