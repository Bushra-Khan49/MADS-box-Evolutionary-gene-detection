# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Nelumbo nucifera (Sacred Lotus)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Nelumbo nucifera* is a basal eudicot that shows a unique combination of ancestral MADS-box stability and lineage-specific expansions.

### **AP1/FUL Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `XP_010255596.1_MADS`, `XP_010272608.1_MADS`, `XP_010255590.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: This clade shows significant expansion relative to basal angiosperms.
*   **Pattern**: **Basal Divergence** identified. `XP_010255590.1_MADS` branches basally (Separate), while the other IDs form a derived group (Together).

### **AG Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `XP_010272685.1_MADS`, `XP_010245525.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: Core floral organ identity genes show the 1:2 duplication pattern characteristic of post-ancestral polyploidy.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **The Nelumbo-specific WGD**
*Nelumbo* belongs to the Proteales, a lineage that diverged early from the rest of the eudicots. It underwent a **lineage-specific whole-genome duplication (WGD)** approximately 65 million years ago. Notably, it lacks the core eudicot $\gamma$ triplication event, confirming its position as a basal eudicot.

### **Why this happens**:
The "Separate vs Together" pattern in the AP1/FUL clade (e.g., `XP_010255590.1` Separate vs the Cluster) represents the genomic outcome of the 65 Mya WGD. One copy likely diverged to manage the specialized aquatic developmental traits of the sacred lotus (e.g., specialized perianth or petaloid stamens), while the "Together" cluster potentially maintained conserved roles in floral induction.

---

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
*Nelumbo* is often called a "living fossil" due to its slow nucleotide substitution rate. Its MADS-box profile confirms this, as even its "Expanded" clades show fewer total copies than the highly active Asterid (*Helianthus*) and Rosid (*Glycine*) lineages, providing a crucial evolutionary baseline for core eudicot studies.
