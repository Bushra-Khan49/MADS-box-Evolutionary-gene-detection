# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Amborella trichopoda (Basal Angiosperm)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Amborella trichopoda* represents the most basal lineage of all living flowering plants. Its MADS-box repertoire is remarkably stable, typically showing 1:1 or 1:2 orthology relative to Arabidopsis anchors.

### **AP1/FUL Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Atrichopoda_0021`, `Atrichopoda_0034`
*   **Observations**: Minimal expansion compared to core eudicots.
*   **Pattern**: No "Separate vs Together" pattern found. Direct orthology to the ancestral-type FUL/AP1 lineage.

### **SEP Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Atrichopoda_0005`, `Atrichopoda_0012`
*   **Observations**: This lineage pre-dates the massive expansion of the SEP3/SEP4 clades seen in core eudicots.

### **SOC1 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Atrichopoda_0001`
*   **Observations**: A single, stable ancestral ortholog of the SOC1 floral transition regulator.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **The Ancestral Angiosperm Status**
The MADS-box genes in *Amborella* provide a "snapshot" of the primitive flower's genetic toolkit. Notably, it lacks the **$\gamma$ (gamma) hexaploidy event** (~120 Mya) that defines the base of the core eudicots. This explains why *Amborella* has significantly fewer paralogs than *Glycine* or *Helianthus*.

### **Why this happens**:
Because *Amborella* did not undergo the subsequent rounds of polyploidy ($\gamma$, $\beta$, $\alpha$) seen in core eudicots like Arabidopsis, its gene families have not been subjected to the same "birth-and-death" cycles of duplication and loss. The sequences we observe represent the ancestral states from which more complex ABCDE networks evolved.

---

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The 1:1 orthology seen in *Amborella*'s SOC1 and AG lineages contrasts sharply with the "Basal Divergence" patterns seen in derived species like *Prunus* and *Glycine*, highlighting the role of post-ancestral polyploidy in generating gene family complexity.
