## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **The Core Eudicot $\gamma$ and Asteraceae WGT-1 Events**
Sunflowers have a highly complex genomic architecture resulting from:
1.  **$\gamma$ (gamma)**: The ancestral core eudicot triplication (~120 Mya).
2.  **WGT-1**: The **Asterid-specific whole genome triplication**, which occurred approximately 65-70 million years ago.
3.  **Heliantheae-specific WGD**: A recent duplication event (~29 Mya).

### **Why this happens**:
The "Separate vs Together" pattern in the SOC1 clade (e.g., `HanXRQChr12g0358711` Separate vs the Cluster) is a visual record of these triplication events. The "Together" cluster represents the massive recent expansion within the Heliantheae tribe, while the "Separate" basal member likely traces back to the earlier WGT-1 or even $\gamma$ events, representing a more ancestral transcriptional regulatory module.

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The extreme "Together" expansion in *Helianthus* exceeds even that of the polyploid legume *Glycine*. This reflects the unique hexaploid history of the Asteraceae, which likely provided the developmental plasticity required to evolve the **pseudanthium** (the composite "flower head" characteristic of sunflowers).
