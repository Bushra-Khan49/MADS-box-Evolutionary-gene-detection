# 5. Evolutionary Conclusion: Prunus persica (Peach)

## 1. Precise Clade Analysis and ID Mapping
*Prunus persica* demonstrates the core eudicot model of MADS-box diversification, showing clear patterns of orthology with Arabidopsis.

### **SVP/AGL24 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Prupe.1G531700.1_MADS`, `Prupe.1G531100.1_MADS`, `Prupe.1G531400.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: This clade is critical for regulating bud dormancy and floral transition.
*   **Pattern**: **Basal Divergence** identified. `Prupe.1G531700.1_MADS` branches basally (Separate), while `Prupe.1G531100.1` and `Prupe.1G531400.1` form a derived group (Together).

### **SOC1 Clade**
*   **Target IDs**: `Prupe.2G287500.1_MADS`
*   **Observations**: Shows 1:1 orthology relative to the Arabidopsis SOC1 gene, indicating high functional conservation.

## 2. Biological Context and WGD Influence
### **The Core Eudicot $\gamma$ (gamma) Event**
All Rosids, including *Prunus*, trace their evolutionary foundation back to the **$\gamma$ (gamma) polyploidy event** (hexaploidy), occurring ~115-120 million years ago. This event tripled the genome at the base of the core eudicots, allowing for the initial radiation of the ABCDE floral homeotic genes.

### **Why this happens**:
The "Separate vs Together" pattern in the SVP-like clade (e.g., `Prupe.1G51700` vs the cluster) marks the functional specialization that followed the $\gamma$ event. The basal "Separate" gene likely diverged to manage environmental sensing (dormancy), a trait crucial for temperate fruit trees, while the derived "Together" cluster potentially acts in traditional floral induction pathways.

---

## 3. Related Findings Across Species
The 1:1 orthology of SOC1 in *Prunus* is very similar to the pattern found in *Amborella* and *Cinnamomum*, indicating that while clades like SVP expand easily through polyploidy, the core SOC1 lineage remains remarkably stable across major angiosperm groups.
